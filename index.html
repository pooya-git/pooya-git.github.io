I received my PhD in Mathematics under supervision of Prof. K. Behrend. Before moving to Vancouver, I was a graduate fellow of the University of Pennsylvania. Prior to my graduate studies, I majored in Computer Science and Mathematics in Sharif University of Technology.

My primary research interest is Algebraic Geometry. I study moduli stacks that are motivated by mathematical string theory. My thesis is a joint work with Kai Behrend on Hall algebras of algebraic stacks. We use the inertia construct of stacks to define a graded structure on the Hall algebra. This work is motivated by fundamental questions in generalized Donaldson-Thomas theory.

I am the Lead of Fundamental Research in 1QB Information Technologies supervising 15 research projects involving 12 mathematicians and computer scientists in research areas related to quantum computation; e.g. optimization theory, combinatorial optimization, and stochastic processes.
